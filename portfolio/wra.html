Workflow Routing Agent: Engineering the Legal "Concierge"

Workday | Agentic AI, B2B SaaS, Web | 2025 | Lead Product Designer

Overview

Product

Establishing a High-Velocity Entry Point for Enterprise Legal Requests — the Workflow Routing Agent is an AI-driven feature that provides an intuitive, standardized entry point for business users to precisely route legal assistance requests.

My roles and responsibilities

Lead Product Designer — 0-to-1 Product Launch & AI Agent Architecture

Team

2 PMs, 1 Sr. Director of Product, Engineering & Data Science Teams

Timeline

Background

The Challenge: The "Front Door" Bottleneck

Before the Workflow Routing Agent, business users faced a fragmented manual intake process that was unsustainable for enterprise scale.

  • Navigation Friction: Users were forced to navigate 400+ contract templates and 50+ contract types without guidance.
  • Operational Waste: Legal teams managed over 3,000 requests via spreadsheets and email, leading to a potential 9% loss in annual turnover due to inefficiency.
  • The "Cold Start" Problem: Non-legal users struggled to identify the correct workflow, causing high drop-off rates and delayed deal conversions.

The Business Impact

I led the design strategy to transform a high-risk manual process into a centralized, revenue-driving "Legal Front Door".

Market Validation

Successfully unlocked new client deals with Fortune 500 companies

Engagement Growth

Drove a 287% increase in questions asked and a 30% increase in unique users

Efficiency Gains

Reduced turnaround time for workflow initiation, directly impacting ARR and deal conversion

Product Strategy & Implementation

1. Centralized Legal Request Hub

I served as the strategic link between executive vision and technical execution to define a standardized entry point for all legal assistance. Users can invoke the routing agent through a central hub accessible via the "Ask AI" interface, consolidating fragmented touchpoints into a single ingress integrated into the broader Workday ecosystem.

Centralized Access: Invoking the Workflow Routing Agent through the centralized Ask AI hub for immediate assistance.

2. Natural Language Routing

I designed an interaction model that translates ambiguous user intent into precise legal actions through conversation. The AI presents options and asks context-aware follow-up questions to guide non-legal users to the correct workflow without requiring technical knowledge, saving significant time previously spent navigating 400+ templates.

Natural Language Interaction: AI-driven dialogue presenting options and context-aware questions to identify the correct contract path.

3. One-Time Configuration for Legal Teams

I architected the configuration layer to empower legal administrators to "program" the agent without technical expertise. This includes a structured UI for setting up agent parameters, testing routing logic, and viewing analytics — ensuring long-term maintenance overhead is minimized.

Agent Configuration: Setting up agent parameters, testing the routing logic, and viewing analytics within the configuration dashboard.

Executive Summary of Impact

  • High-Margin Growth: Contributed to the "Ask AI" feature suite, currently a primary driver for Evisort's B2B SaaS revenue.
  • Operational Scale: Shifted the organization from a manual 3,000-request backlog to an automated, intelligent routing system.
  • Platform Maturity: Established foundational patterns for natural language routing that now serve as a blueprint for the Workday CLM Evolution.
